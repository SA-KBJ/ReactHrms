Introduction

This Employee HandBook is created to bring the clarification about the terms and procedure of the organization which need to be followed and maintained by employees. This small hand book will help you to a great extent to establish/understand the culture at Solution Analysts and make you align with your team at the grass-roots level, and organization at the highest level.

 

This handbook will be released every Year. All interim policy changes communicated from time to time in between the two releases will be incorporated in the next release. We welcome your suggestions and inputs to make this manual more effective.

 

The policy will be owned by HR who will be responsible for making suitable amendments, prescribed & suggested by management, if any, from time to time.

Terms of Employment

It is in the interest of the Individual’s and the organization’s growth that all employees devote their time and attention exclusively to the duties entrusted to them. We expect our employees not to engage directly or indirectly for any person, firm or company other than Solution Analysts and its group of companies in any capacity whatsoever without obtaining prior permission of the Management of the company in writing. This service will be subject to the rules and regulations of the company as may be framed or amended from time to time.

 

(A) Work Hours: Normal working hours of the company in India are from 10:00 AM to 7:00 PM from Monday to Friday. The lunch break is given for 1 hour which can be taken at any time after 12:30 pm. We have off on 1st, 3rd and 5th Saturdays of the month. 2nd & 4th Saturday hours are 08:00 AM to 01:00 PM for training and self development. If no training/other activity/deliverable is planned, it will be considered as off day. If it is planned by your leaders and you do not appear, it will be considered as leave. This leave needs to be approved.

 

(B)  Documents: An employee is required to submit all the documents related to his qualification, past employment and any other document as requested and asked by the HR department.

 

  • Educational & Professional Documents: Photocopy of highest degree certificate; with professional documents like photocopy of last organization appointment letter, experience & reliving letter, last 3 months salary slips along with bank statement needs to be submitted at the time of joining.

 

  • Passport: In the light of the nature of our business, all employees, specially the technical employees, are advised to have a passport. However if one doesn’t have the same, he/she is advised to get it made and provide the photocopy of the same to the HR department within 6 months of joining. This is in the Interest of the employee and the company.

 

  • Driving license: All employees are advised to submit the copy of their driving license as a valid proof of identity. If an employee doesn’t possess a driving license, he/she is advised to get it made and provide the photocopy of the same to HR department within 3 months of joining.

 

  • PAN Card: All employees are advised to submit the PAN Card copy as this is the essential proof for processing salary, opening the bank account & for filing the income tax returns.

 

(C)  Probation Period : The probation period of an employee in the organization would be 3 months from the Date of Joining. There will be a confirmation email sent from HR Department to the employee for confirming the employment in the organization without which the confirmation of employment won’t be effective. It’s employee’s responsibility to check with HR Department in case the mail is not received by him/her. The probation period may be curtailed or extended by the Company at its sole discretion based on individual’s performance.

 

(D) Notice Period: Every Employee has to serve at least Sixty days notice period from the date of resignation which can be extended further if the need arises or if the allocated task or project is not completed. The employee is not allowed to take any leave when serving notice period. If at all the employee has to take leave, the no. of days of leave taken would be extended and added to the notice period days. Further, employee has to complete the knowledge transfer of Project, which has been assigned to him/her, during the course of employment. In case, one does not serve notice period of Sixty days and do not complete the knowledge transfer of assigned project, before leaving job, then he/she is liable to pay sixty days salary in lieu of that. On failure to pay the above said amount, he/she is also liable to pay interest @ 12% p.a., on this amount from the date it becomes due, till the date of actual payment. If notice is not provided in accordance with this policy, SA will not pay out the employee’s salary.

 

Code of Conduct

Code/rules of conduct and behavior are necessary within all organizations not only in order to develop good business practices, but also create a very harmonious eco-system which becomes enabler for the growth of every team member. In establishing these rules, the Company has no intention of restricting the legal rights of any individual. Rather, we wish to define the guidelines to ensure maximum understanding and cooperation.

 

Violation of the guidelines below may result in disciplinary action, and the Company may be constrained to terminate the services of an employee. The level of discipline will be determined at the Company’s discretion considering the frequency and severity of the offense(s).

 

Dress Code:

While Solution Analysts does not wish to limit the employee’s expression of taste and individuality, we expect our employees to dress professionally and decently.

 

Eco-consciousness:

  • Switch off lights and AC’s when you leave the meeting rooms and workplace. The world is gearing up to go green, and it is our moral responsibility to contribute to save planet earth.
  • Avoid using paper, as long as you can use softcopy. Paper comes at the cost of trees. Help us preserve trees.
  • Save water. Close the taps properly after using wash rooms.
  • Clean your desk and surrounding areas; keep practice of using dustbins.

 

Communication:

  • English is the only language of communication in Information Technology industry, and good command over the same enhances our acceptability with our customer.

 

Confidentiality:

  • We expect that our employees will maintain the confidentiality, and will not disclose any details pertaining to the employment, customers and the projects without the written consent of the Company. Disclosure of sensitive information about our Clients or Projects is not only unethical, but also illegal. It can have a negative impact on our business reputation and our trustworthiness; which could possibly have far reaching legal implications with the concerned clients.

 

  • If any employee releases or reveals any kind of information especially with respect to the Clients, Projects and/ or Solution Analysts, or shares any training related information or material through emails, profiles on the web or in any public forums or social networking sites without prior authorization, it may result into termination of employment, as well as suitable legal/penal/other action.

 

Conflict of interest:

The Company expects all employees to avoid activities that create conflicts of interest towards their responsibilities to this Company. Conflicts of interest may include, but are not limited to:

 

  • Outside employment: with a competitor, supplier or any other entity, whether formal or informal, remunerative or non remunerative
  • Legal requirements: Employees should not do anything in the conduct of business, which would violate any local, state or federal law.
  • Financial Speculation: Employees should not speculate in materials, supplies or services produced or purchased by this company.

 

Mobile Etiquettes:

Keep the mobile in silent or vibrator mode during office hours. Mobile phones should not be left unattended. Personal calls during office hours should be minimal and short.

 

General Office Etiquettes:

  • Personal work should be attended to during non-business hours. All customers and employees of the Company must be treated in a courteous, professional and respectful manner.
  • Any damage to the Company or customer property must be reported immediately to the appropriate supervisor. The cost of repair, in such cases, will have to be borne by the concerned employee.
  • Introducing or copying personal software onto the computer system of the Company or the customer is prohibited. Similarly, copying any company or project related information onto any external media, or sending the same out by any electronics media outside office to the people not concerned with the work/project is strictly prohibited.
  • Personal use of the computer including use of the Internet for personal work during working hours is not allowed.
  • Please keep your voices low on the floor at all the times. If any discussion needs to be done, please use the meeting room if less than 5 members are involved in the discussion and conference room if more than 5 members are involved in the discussion.
  • Kindly refrain from using the meeting rooms or conference room to attend personal phone calls.
  • Employees are requested to refrain from visiting any prohibited sites such as adult-sites and personal email accounts (Gmail, Yahoo etc) and social media for personal purpose.
  • Employees should always ensure that the business information contained in email/Internet messages is accurate, appropriate and lawful. Specifically, usage related to racially derogatory, discriminating, threatening or abusive communication is prohibited.
  • Kindly don’t make any disparaging remarks about, or to, any employee of the Company or any of its customers concerning sex, race, religion, national origin, age, disability, or any other legally protected status.
  • Employees are requested not to take their family members, friends or personal visitors inside the company premises beyond the Reception Area. If at all required, seek for prior permission from management.

Compensation/Salary

While job satisfaction is made up of many factors in addition to the amount of your paycheck, salary is important. Our salary policy is designed to compensate employees on a fair and equitable basis.

You will get your salary deposited in your account on or before 5th of every month. The company has its salary deposited with just ICICI Bank. If you already have your account in the mentioned bank, then it will be deposited directly in your that account after you provide account number and if you don’t have account in the mentioned bank, then company will open up a new account for you. Employee Benefits & Leaves Entitlement

Impact on the business is the first and foremost criteria while approving the leaves which will be decided by the management.

(A)  Overview & Total Leaves Structure

 

  1. Every Employee is entitled to get 18 days leave per year (i.e. 1.5 days per month and employee has to earn these).
  2. From above leaves, maximum 6 Leaves can be accumulated every year up to 36 days in entire service period. The encashment can be done after completion of 3 years of service with the discretion of the Management and will be prorated based on basic salary.
  3. Maximum 36 leaves can be carried forward up to two years. And one can avail maximum 2 (TWO) weeks of leaves from your available leave balance at a stretch.
  4. 10 Public holidays including Floating Leave will be declared at the starting of the year and if you are representing a customer, it is required to inform customer well in advance. (Please check the Holiday calendar listed in PMS)
  5. 1 Floating leave will be sanctioned on any of the birthday/ anniversary/ religious festival/ social functions.
  6. When leave is taken in some emergency circumstances, the absence should be notified to the Department Head on the same day through phone. Approved leave application should reach to the HR department within the day of rejoining.
  7. Any Absenteeism in office for more than 4 hours during regular working days will be considered as half day.
  8. Uninformed leave or Unapproved leave (explained as below) will be considered as 2 days leave and salary would be deducted accordingly.
    1. Uninformed leave - If in case you are absent and if leaves are not applied for, then this would invoke deduction in salary twice for those numbers of days.
    2. Unapproved leave - In case of leaves being ‘Unapproved’ where the employees have still taken the leave, will invoke deduction in salary twice for those numbers of days.

The leaves should be informed to & approved by the Department Head only. You can contact HR Department to know your Department head.

 (B)  Maternity & Paternity Leave

  • Married female employees who are on permanent role shall be entitled to maternity leave as per the Maternity Benefit (Amendment) Act, 1995: 84 days of paid Maternity Leave is allowed to females who have to deliver a child and 42 days in case of miscarriage. The maternity leave benefit can be availed only two times during the entire service.
  • 2 days of Paternity leaves can be availed & only two times during the entire service.
  • Before availing this leaves, a certificate from the gynecologist has to be submitted mentioning the expected date of delivery. Intervening Public / weekly off days will be counted as part of leave.
  • If, because of any complication, leave has to be extended, it can be done with prior approval of management and will fall under LWP.

 

(C)  Marriage Leave

  • Applicant can take maximum of 21 days leaves once during the entire service period with prior approval from management.
  • These leaves can be availed maximum of 2 weeks at a stretch. Applicant can use total quota of the leaves (Total Yearly Leaves) at a stretch from the available balance & in the absence of these; leaves will be considered as LWP.

 

(D)  Leave without Pay (LWP)

  • LWP will be applicable in the case of Zero leave balance.
  • If any employee fails to report on the specified date after leaves taken, it will be considered as LWP.
  • LWP can be implicated on disciplinary grounds with regard to attendance by the management.

 

(E) Compensatory Off

  • If an employee is required to work on any Public holiday / weekly off day, is eligible for Compensatory off.
  • Official approval will be required from the Management. Only two days of compensatory offs can be combined and availed at a stretch. The compensatory off has to be availed within a period of three months from the date of eligibility.
  • No compensatory offs will be entertained in such cases like project/client exigency, and low productivity during employment period or notice period.

 

(F) Work From Home:

  • Work from home policy is applicable only to the employee who is directly engaged in client communication and also subject to prior approval.
  • One must seek approval from the management one week in advance. They must have internet connection at home. And must drop an email to all concern people about his/her plan for the day; at the end of the day, what he/she has achieved.
  •  Work from home without prior approval will be considered as leave. Work from home is not allowed when you are not engaged in the project.

(G) Procedure for Leave Application:

  • The leaves will be applied & managed through HRMS system and approved by Department Head. Leaves should be applied only in the given format of the system; otherwise will be considered as void and will be cancelled.
  • In case, of more than 2 days of leaves requirement, employee has to plan & apply at least one month in advance. You need to plan one day leave at least one week in advance. (Exception is sick leave or unavoidable circumstances like accident, death, or emergency cases).
  • Status of leave tickets applied for more than 2 days will be updated by management in maximum of 10 days from the date of application. It’s your responsibility for having an approved leave from manager in the system before availing the leave.

Appraisal

A. Performance Feedback

Performance feedback of all the employees will be done twice a year.(i.e. Every April & October) which will be shared to all employees with required suggestions, notices and appreciation by HR department & management team.

B. Increment

Performance appraisal will be done once in a year in the month of April to appraise the work of all

employees, for their salary increment & promotions. The employees who have completed 4 months or more as of 31st March will be eligible for the annual appraisal. The salary increment for employees, who have not completed one year with the company on March 31st, will be done on a pro-rata basis.

C. Incident Tracking System

Incident Tracking System allows us to file an incident and keep the track on for Individual performance & productivity. Incident can be for appreciation as well as for areas of improvement. Incident can be filled by anybody (delivery team, support staff & management team). Incident email will be tracked and maintained by HR Department & will be reviewed & guided by management team. Tickets needs to be filed on the basis of format shared in the HRMS System.

Referral Program

To facilitate the hiring of talented candidates and to encourage and reward employees for recommending qualified candidates.

 

  • For Fresher/ Trainee: N/A
  • For Junior Engineer (1 to 3 Years): INR 5,000
  • For Senior Engineer (3 to 5 years): INR 7,000
  • For Managerial & leading Positions (Above 5 Years) :INR 12,000

 

Procedure & Eligibility:


a) Employee should email the soft copy of the resume (of the referred candidate) to HR. This would be logged in the HR Database.


b) Employee will be informed about the final outcome of the candidate in interview process.

 

c) No referral bonuses are paid for referrals of candidates who have previously worked for The Company, such as retires or rehires, or persons returning from a leave of absence.


d) Referral Bonus will be given to the employee only when candidate has completed six months from the date of joining. To be eligible for the referral bonus described herein, both the referred candidate and the employee must be on the Company payroll at the time of disbursement of referral bonus.

Criticisms and Grievances

 

We welcome constructive criticism on the part of every member of our company. Airing a grievance with a fellow worker often enhances the problem, whereas discussion of the same problem with HR Department may bring about a speedy and satisfactory solution. We expect your cooperation in this matter. No employee may be subject to retaliation or reprisal for airing a criticism or grievance with the HR Department.
